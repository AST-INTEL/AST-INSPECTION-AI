## Why This Phase Exists

No assumptions. Before writing a single line of pipeline code, we need to know exactly what data we have, in what format, in what condition. Data preparation surprises in Phase 2 can unravel everything built in Phase 1. The audit exists to surface those surprises now, when they are cheap to address.

The output of this phase is a written data inventory document committed to the repository — not a mental model, a documented artifact.

## Image Inventory

<Steps>
1. **Count total images** across the entire inspection archive — exact number, not estimate
2. **Classify by sensor type** — RGB wide, RGB zoom, thermal IR — establish the split
3. **Verify GPS metadata** — ExifTool audit on a sample of 50+ images, check for missing/corrupt EXIF data
4. **Check thermal format** — confirm whether thermals are RJPEG (DJI proprietary, requires SDK extraction) or standard JPEG
5. **Assess naming convention** — is there a consistent naming scheme? Are images linkable to specific jobs?
6. **Identify outliers** — corrupted files, wrong format, extreme resolution outliers, zero-byte files
</Steps>

**Tools:** ExifTool, Python (PIL/Pillow for batch validation), DJI Thermal SDK

---

## Report Inventory

<Steps>
1. **Count total reports** in the archive
2. **Format split** — native PDF vs scanned PDF vs Word (.docx) — percentage of each
3. **Language split** — English only, Arabic only, bilingual — percentage of each
4. **Report-to-image linkage** — can reports be matched to their corresponding image sets by job ID, date, or naming convention?
5. **Completeness check** — are there jobs with images but no report? Reports with no corresponding images?
6. **Document the worst cases** — lowest quality scans, most inconsistent formatting — these determine OCR strategy
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Arabic OCR is a known difficulty">
Arabic text in scanned PDFs is significantly harder for OCR than Latin text. The proportion of scanned Arabic reports determines how much PaddleOCR accuracy work is needed in Phase 2. Do not underestimate this.
</Aside>

## Defect Frequency Analysis

**Goal:** Before training the vision model, know what we will be training it to find.

From the existing report archive, extract a frequency count of all defect types mentioned. This gives us the initial defect taxonomy for Phase 3 — grounded in real CANAR data, not guesswork.

Expected output: A ranked list of defect types by frequency, which directly informs which defect classes to prioritise in the labeling effort.
